Publications

Mechanistic Interpretability of Transformer Attention Patterns

2024

Alexandria Chen, Michael Zhang, Sarah Johnson

Proceedings of ICML 2024 | International Conference on Machine Learning

We present a novel framework for understanding how transformer attention heads specialize in different linguistic tasks. Through activation analysis and probing experiments, we demonstrate that attention patterns follow interpretable circuits.

📄 arXiv:2024.12345

Efficient Vision Transformers with Pruned Attention

2023

Alexandria Chen, David Liu, Priya Sharma

IEEE/CVF Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR) 2023

We introduce a structured pruning method that removes redundant attention heads in vision transformers, achieving 40% parameter reduction with minimal accuracy loss across multiple vision benchmarks.

📄 arXiv:2023.54321

Preventing Catastrophic Forgetting in Continual Learning

2023

Alexandria Chen, Elena Rodriguez, James Wilson

Neural Information Processing Systems (NeurIPS) 2023

We propose a memory-augmented approach combining experience replay with dynamic task boundaries. Our method outperforms existing continual learning approaches on multiple sequential task benchmarks.

📄 arXiv:2023.11111

Adversarial Robustness through Certified Defenses

2022

Alexandria Chen, Robert Chen, Lisa Park

Journal of Machine Learning Research (JMLR) Vol. 45, 2022

We develop certified defense mechanisms using randomized smoothing and Lipschitz-constrained networks. Our approach provides formal robustness guarantees against ℓ2 perturbations.

📄 arXiv:2022.99999

Attention is All You Need to Explain

2022

Sarah Johnson, Alexandria Chen, Michael Zhang

ACL Findings 2022 | Association for Computational Linguistics

This work demonstrates that attention weights alone provide meaningful explanations for NLP model predictions. We validate this through extensive human evaluation studies across multiple languages.

📄 arXiv:2022.88888

Neural Basis of Selective Attention in Deep Networks

2021

Alexandria Chen, James Wilson, Elena Gutierrez

Cognitive Computational Neuroscience Conference (CCN) 2021

We analyze the computational principles underlying selective attention in neural networks and compare them with findings from neuroscience, revealing surprising similarities in information filtering mechanisms.

📄 arXiv:2021.77777
